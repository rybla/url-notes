Why did air conditioning spread so fast in Mexico? - Marginal REVOLUTION
economicstechnologymexicoair conditioningclimateeconomic modelingforecastingenergy prices
  • A 2015 model predicting air conditioning (AC) adoption in Mexico based on income and temperature has been found to have significantly underestimated growth.
  • The prediction gap is not explained by errors in forecasting income growth, temperature increases, migration patterns, or model restrictions.
  • The primary drivers for the accelerated adoption were falling electricity prices and technological improvements in AC efficiency, which were not accounted for in the original model.
  • As of 2022, Mexico's AC penetration reached approximately 18.5%.

  • Problem: The American "can-do" spirit is hindered by bureaucratic and legal obstacles, requiring systemic reform to restore efficient project execution and decision-making.
  • Proposed Solution: A three-pronged approach involving a new legal framework, a new trust-building institution, and a special commission to design the implementation details.
  • Legal Framework Reforms:
    • Centralized Authority: Designate a single lead agency for each infrastructure category, with ultimate approval authority subject to White House oversight. Federal decisions would preempt state/local approvals for nationally significant projects.

The Future of Art with AI - Econlib
aiarteconomicslaw of demandmarginal utilityimperfect substitutionmarket bifurcationlabor marketfuture of workautomationcreative industriescomparative advantage
  • Law of Demand: As AI lowers the price of creating art, the quantity of art demanded will increase.
  • Diminishing Marginal Utility: The increased demand will primarily serve low-marginal-benefit uses (e.g., personal jokes, blog post illustrations) that were previously not worth the cost of hiring an artist. This creates a new market rather than displacing jobs.
  • Imperfect Substitution: For high-marginal-use art (e.g., commercial products, custom portraits), AI is an imperfect substitute for human artists due to difficulties in communication, interpretation, and achieving specific, nuanced results.
  • Market Bifurcation: The art market is expected to split:
    • A low-marginal-use segment dominated by AI.
    • A high-marginal-use segment where human artists maintain a comparative advantage.
  • Job Impact: Job losses will be concentrated on "intramarginal" artists, whose work is most directly substitutable by AI's capabilities. The overall impact is not predicted to be an existential crisis for the art industry.

Should Strong Gods Bet On GDP?
politicseconomicssociologycommunityliberalismpolitical philosophystrong godsintentional communitiesfukuyamagdpeconomic growthpost-scarcityuniversal basic incomecultural criticismsocial organization
  • Thesis: Liberalism, as articulated by Fukuyama, is intended as a platform for diverse, voluntary, "strong god" communities to flourish, rather than imposing a single one.
  • Critique: The author argues this model is failing in practice; less than 10% of Americans belong to such intentional communities, with most people defaulting to a mainstream culture they claim to dislike.
  • Hypothesis: The primary inhibitor to forming these communities is economic constraint. Most individuals cannot afford to disengage from the mainstream economy to build or join alternative social structures.
  • Evidence: The author analyzes successful modern communities (e.g., Amish, Ultra-Orthodox Jews, Rationalists), concluding that financial resources (or extreme commitment) are a key factor in their ability to self-organize, co-locate, and fund community projects.
  • Conclusion: Material abundance (high GDP) and strong communities are not opposing but complementary goals. Post-scarcity economics (e.g., UBI after a singularity) could remove the financial barriers that currently prevent people from opting out of mainstream culture and forming their own meaningful communities.

Ask HN: How can ChatGPT serve 700M users when I can't run one GPT-4 locally?
llmlarge language modelchatgptgpt-4inferencescalabilityhardwaregputpuparallelismbatchingmemory bandwidthmodel optimizationmixture of expertsmoequantizationspeculative decodingkv cacheeconomicsinfrastructurehacker news

  • Batching & Parallelism:

    • Large-scale inference relies on batching, where multiple user requests are processed simultaneously.
    • The primary bottleneck in LLM inference is memory bandwidth (loading model weights from VRAM to compute units).
    • By batching requests, weights are loaded once and applied to many queries, amortizing the high cost of memory access and dramatically increasing throughput.
    • This process is highly parallelizable across large clusters of GPUs.

  • Hardware & Infrastructure:

    • Massive investment in specialized hardware like NVIDIA H100s or Google TPUs, which feature large amounts of high-bandwidth memory (HBM).
    • Models are sharded (split) across multiple GPUs (tensor and pipeline parallelism), so a single model runs on a cluster of interconnected accelerators.
    • The infrastructure is built for high utilization, unlike a local setup which is mostly idle.

  • Model Optimizations:

    • Mixture of Experts (MoE): Models are designed so that only a fraction of the total parameters (the "experts") are activated for any given token, reducing the computational cost per inference.
    • Quantization: Model weights are compressed to lower-precision formats (e.g., INT8, FP8) to reduce memory footprint and bandwidth requirements.
    • Speculative Decoding: A smaller, faster "draft" model generates candidate tokens which are then validated in parallel by the larger, more powerful model, increasing token generation speed.
    • KV Cache: The key-value state of the attention mechanism is cached, so the context doesn't need to be re-processed for each new token generated in a sequence.

  • Economic Model:

    • The operation is funded by billions of dollars in investment, allowing companies to run at a loss to capture market share.
    • The cost per user is manageable because of the efficiencies of scale and because the vast majority of users are idle most of the time, allowing for high multi-tenancy on the hardware.
