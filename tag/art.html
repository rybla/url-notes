The Future of Art with AI - Econlib
aiarteconomicslaw of demandmarginal utilityimperfect substitutionmarket bifurcationlabor marketfuture of workautomationcreative industriescomparative advantage
  • Law of Demand: As AI lowers the price of creating art, the quantity of art demanded will increase.
  • Diminishing Marginal Utility: The increased demand will primarily serve low-marginal-benefit uses (e.g., personal jokes, blog post illustrations) that were previously not worth the cost of hiring an artist. This creates a new market rather than displacing jobs.
  • Imperfect Substitution: For high-marginal-use art (e.g., commercial products, custom portraits), AI is an imperfect substitute for human artists due to difficulties in communication, interpretation, and achieving specific, nuanced results.
  • Market Bifurcation: The art market is expected to split:
    • A low-marginal-use segment dominated by AI.
    • A high-marginal-use segment where human artists maintain a comparative advantage.
  • Job Impact: Job losses will be concentrated on "intramarginal" artists, whose work is most directly substitutable by AI's capabilities. The overall impact is not predicted to be an existential crisis for the art industry.
28 slightly rude notes on writing
writingcreativitythinkingphilosophy of writingartificial intelligenceaimeaningauthenticitycommunicationcraftartemotionlegacycontextsincerity
  • Motive and Cost: Effective writing requires a strong, intrinsic "motive." The writer must answer "Why do you care?" not just "Why should the reader care?" This act of writing is a costly, and therefore meaningful, signal of that care, a quality absent in zero-cost AI-generated text.
  • Thinking vs. Writing: Writing problems are fundamentally thinking problems. Clarity of expression cannot be achieved without clarity of thought. The goal is to operate in the liminal space between the obvious and the obviously wrong.
  • Context over Content: The meaning of text ("the necklace") is derived from its human context ("the neck")â€”the writer's specific circumstances, intent, and sacrifice. AI-generated prose, lacking this "coming-from-ness," is all necklace, no neck.
  • The Nature of Creation: The creative process is described as an unnatural and painful act of forcing precision from the mind. This pain can be addictive, making writing a compulsion for some.
  • Emotion and Sincerity: Bitterness is a sterile emotion for writing because it is self-absorbed. In contrast, writing from despair is an inherent act of hope, a subtextual plea for connection.
  • Legacy and Misinterpretation: A writer's legacy is often outside their control; they are frequently misunderstood or remembered for works they deemed secondary.
  • AI as a Catalyst: Competition from AI presents an opportunity for human writers to focus on their unique advantages: exploring the ineffable, personal experience and "widening the sphere of human sensibility."
Modern Culture Trends
sociologyculturemodernitysocial changeeconomicstechnologypoliticspsychologyindividualismsecularismrationalismegalitarianismcapitalismconsumerismglobalizationurbanizationsocial normssignaling theoryartmediafamilygenderworkeducation

  • Learning & Information:

    • Decreased belief in supernatural phenomena; increased trust in science and experts.
    • Shift in purity rituals to focus on germ theory.
    • Increased focus on psychological interiors in fiction and morality.

  • Economic & Technological Development:

    • Adoption of precise timekeeping, measurement, and naming systems.
    • Shift from self-production to a consumer economy with increased wage labor.
    • Increased population density, mobility, and long-distance communication, weakening local and kin-based ties.
    • Growth of larger, for-profit organizations and more capable, regulatory governments.
    • Formal education displacing child labor, fostering abstract and analytical thinking.
    • Market and state insurance displacing informal risk-sharing networks.

  • Egalitarianism:

    • Rejection of social hierarchies based on inherited or group characteristics (family, gender, race, etc.).
    • Repudiation of slavery, colonialism, and aristocracy in favor of democracy.
    • Increased sympathy for rebellion against authority.

  • Effort Minimization (Lazy/Selfish/Myopic):

    • Reduced work, delayed marriage, and fewer children.
    • Deregulation of personal expression and social interaction norms.
    • De-emphasis on self-control; increased acceptance of promiscuity and recreational drugs.
    • Prioritization of individual comfort and happiness over community duty and honor.

  • Signaling:

    • Cost-ineffective behaviors to signal positive traits (e.g., over-insurance to show care, over-consumption to show wealth, excessive schooling to show conscientiousness).
    • Following fashion and news to signal social connection.
    • Seeking novelty and variety to signal authenticity and creativity.

  • Non-adaptive Enjoyment:

    • Mate selection based on personal choice rather than arrangement.
    • Increased consumption of fiction and music.
    • Over-eating and expression of more gendered personalities.
    • Wealth-driven paternalism and cultural activism as a form of status-seeking.

  • System: A search engine for locating text within Google Street View images of New York City.
  • Core Technology: Utilizes Optical Character Recognition (OCR) to extract textual data from image sources.
  • Data Source: Leverages Google Street View imagery captured between 2007 and 2024 across all five NYC boroughs.
  • Scope: Primarily processes and indexes text in English and other languages using the Latin alphabet.
  • Functionality: Enables users to search for words or phrases, generating a "route" of their occurrences throughout the city's visual landscape (e.g., signs, graffiti).
  • Stated Purpose: Intended for educational, artistic, and research applications.
