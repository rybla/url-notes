GPT-5: Key characteristics, pricing and model card
aigpt-5large language modelsopenaimachine learningtechnologyapipricingai safetyprompt injectionreasoningmultimodality

  • Model Architecture:

    • ChatGPT Interface: A hybrid system using a real-time router to select between a "smart and fast" model for most queries and a "deeper reasoning" model for complex problems. A "mini" version is used when usage limits are reached.
    • API Access: Offered as three distinct models: gpt-5 (regular), gpt-5-mini, and gpt-5-nano.
    • Reasoning Levels: Each API model can be configured with four reasoning levels: minimal, low, medium, or high.

  • Technical Specifications:

    • Context Window: 272,000 input tokens.
    • Output Limit: 128,000 tokens (includes "invisible" reasoning tokens).
    • Modalities: Supports text and image input; text-only output.
    • Knowledge Cutoff: September 30, 2024 for GPT-5; May 30, 2024 for mini and nano versions.

  • Pricing (per million tokens):

    • GPT-5: $1.25 input / $10.00 output.
    • GPT-5 Mini: $0.25 input / $2.00 output.
    • GPT-5 Nano: $0.05 input / $0.40 output.
    • A 90% discount is available for cached input tokens.

  • Training & Safety:

    • Improvements: Focused on reducing hallucinations, sycophancy, and improving instruction following, particularly for writing, coding, and health queries.
    • Safe-Completions: A new safety training approach that provides moderated, safe answers instead of binary refusals for potentially dual-use prompts.
    • Deception Mitigation: The model is rewarded for admitting when a task is infeasible or when tools (e.g., web browsing) are unreliable.

  • Prompt Injection:

    • Red-teaming resulted in a 56.8% attack success rate (with up to 10 attempts) against gpt-5-thinking.
    • While an improvement over other models, this indicates prompt injection remains an unsolved vulnerability.

  • API Features:

    • Thinking Traces: Reasoning steps can be accessed in the API response by setting "reasoning": {"summary": "auto"}.
    • Latency Control: A reasoning_effort=minimal parameter is available to disable most reasoning and reduce response latency.
Why did air conditioning spread so fast in Mexico? - Marginal REVOLUTION
economicstechnologymexicoair conditioningclimateeconomic modelingforecastingenergy prices
  • A 2015 model predicting air conditioning (AC) adoption in Mexico based on income and temperature has been found to have significantly underestimated growth.
  • The prediction gap is not explained by errors in forecasting income growth, temperature increases, migration patterns, or model restrictions.
  • The primary drivers for the accelerated adoption were falling electricity prices and technological improvements in AC efficiency, which were not accounted for in the original model.
  • As of 2022, Mexico's AC penetration reached approximately 18.5%.

Modern Culture Trends
sociologyculturemodernitysocial changeeconomicstechnologypoliticspsychologyindividualismsecularismrationalismegalitarianismcapitalismconsumerismglobalizationurbanizationsocial normssignaling theoryartmediafamilygenderworkeducation

  • Learning & Information:

    • Decreased belief in supernatural phenomena; increased trust in science and experts.
    • Shift in purity rituals to focus on germ theory.
    • Increased focus on psychological interiors in fiction and morality.

  • Economic & Technological Development:

    • Adoption of precise timekeeping, measurement, and naming systems.
    • Shift from self-production to a consumer economy with increased wage labor.
    • Increased population density, mobility, and long-distance communication, weakening local and kin-based ties.
    • Growth of larger, for-profit organizations and more capable, regulatory governments.
    • Formal education displacing child labor, fostering abstract and analytical thinking.
    • Market and state insurance displacing informal risk-sharing networks.

  • Egalitarianism:

    • Rejection of social hierarchies based on inherited or group characteristics (family, gender, race, etc.).
    • Repudiation of slavery, colonialism, and aristocracy in favor of democracy.
    • Increased sympathy for rebellion against authority.

  • Effort Minimization (Lazy/Selfish/Myopic):

    • Reduced work, delayed marriage, and fewer children.
    • Deregulation of personal expression and social interaction norms.
    • De-emphasis on self-control; increased acceptance of promiscuity and recreational drugs.
    • Prioritization of individual comfort and happiness over community duty and honor.

  • Signaling:

    • Cost-ineffective behaviors to signal positive traits (e.g., over-insurance to show care, over-consumption to show wealth, excessive schooling to show conscientiousness).
    • Following fashion and news to signal social connection.
    • Seeking novelty and variety to signal authenticity and creativity.

  • Non-adaptive Enjoyment:

    • Mate selection based on personal choice rather than arrangement.
    • Increased consumption of fiction and music.
    • Over-eating and expression of more gendered personalities.
    • Wealth-driven paternalism and cultural activism as a form of status-seeking.

I Tried Every Todo App and Ended Up With a .txt File - Alireza Bashiri
productivitytask managementsimplicityminimalismplain textsoftwaretechnologyworkflowsoftware philosophypersonal knowledge managementtodo list.txt filetext-based toolsdigital minimalismfuture-proofingefficiency
  • Problem: Productivity applications often introduce overhead, feature-creep, and subscription costs, leading to more time spent managing the system than completing tasks.
  • Solution: A single todo.txt file for task management.
  • Methodology:
    • Create daily entries with a date stamp (e.g., 2025-08-11).
    • List tasks for the day. Scheduled items are prefixed with a time.
    • Completed tasks are deleted or annotated with outcomes.
    • The file becomes a searchable, chronological log of work and decisions.
  • Key Advantages:
    • Longevity & Portability: Plain text is a universal, future-proof format.
    • Speed & Efficiency: Near-instantaneous to open and edit; no UI lag.
    • Zero-Dependency: Not reliant on any company, API, or subscription.
    • Simplicity: Forces focus on execution rather than organization.
    • Searchable: Utilizes native OS/editor text search.
    • AI-Augmented (Optional): Can be enhanced by modern text editors but is not required.
  • Counter-Arguments:
    • Reminders: Handled by a dedicated calendar application.
    • Projects: Managed with simple text tags (e.g., [PROJECT]).
    • Collaboration: Uses dedicated work-specific tools.
    • Mobile Access: Achieved via file syncing services (e.g., Dropbox).
