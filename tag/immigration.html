Federal Court to Hear Final Arguments in Case Challenging Trump’s Policy of Deporting Student Protesters
lawpoliticsus politicstrump administrationimmigrationdeportationhigher educationstudent rightsfree speechfirst amendmentactivismpro-palestinian advocacymiddle east studiescourt casefederal courtbostonmassachusetts

BOSTON—Lawyers for the American Association of University Professors (AAUP) and the Middle East Studies Association (MESA) are set to deliver closing arguments this morning in a case challenging the Trump administration’s policy of arresting, detaining, and deporting noncitizen faculty and students for their pro-Palestinian advocacy. American Association of University Professors v. Rubio
lawlegalfirst amendmentfree speechacademic freedomhigher educationactivismpalestinian rightsimmigrationconstitutional lawadministrative procedure actknight instituteaaup
  • Case: American Association of University Professors v. Rubio, No. 1:25-cv-10685 (D.Mass), 25-1658 (1st Cir.).
  • Subject: A lawsuit filed by the Knight Institute on behalf of the AAUP and other academic organizations challenging the Trump administration's policy of arresting, detaining, and deporting noncitizen students and faculty for pro-Palestinian activism.
  • Plaintiffs' Arguments:
    • The policy violates the First Amendment right to hear from and associate with noncitizen academics.
    • The policy is unconstitutionally vague.
    • The policy violates the Administrative Procedure Act.
    • The actions create a chilling effect on academic speech and activism.
  • Status:
    • March 25, 2025: Complaint filed.
    • April 29, 2025: District court largely denied the government's motion to dismiss.
    • July 7, 2025: Trial scheduled to begin.

Post-Trial Brief Supplies Comprehensive Account of Trump Administration’s Arrests, Detentions, and Threatened Deportation of Student Protesters
lawpoliticsfirst amendmentfree speechacademic freedomimmigrationdeportationpro-palestinian advocacyknight first amendment institutetrump administrationcivil rightshigher educationus politicslegal case
  • Subject: Post-trial brief filed by the Knight First Amendment Institute in American Association of University Professors v. Rubio.
  • Core Challenge: The case contests a Trump administration policy that arrests, detains, and deports noncitizen academics for pro-Palestinian advocacy.
  • Plaintiff's Argument: The policy constitutes "ideological deportation," targeting individuals for lawful political expression in violation of the First Amendment. The brief alleges the administration characterized pro-Palestinian speech as antisemitic and "pro-Hamas" to silence dissent.
  • Defendant's Argument: Department of Justice lawyers disputed the proposition that noncitizens lawfully admitted to the U.S. are fully protected by the First Amendment.
  • Status: The brief was filed following a two-week trial in the U.S. District Court for the District of Massachusetts. A ruling is pending.
