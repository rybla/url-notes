The Effects of Tenure-Track Systems on Selection and Productivity in Economics
published: D:20250826100247Z00'00'
  • Subject: Analysis of a 2010 Italian reform that replaced permanent assistant professor (AP) positions with fixed-term, publication-based tenure-track contracts in Economics.
  • Methodology: A Difference-in-Differences Event-Study comparing Economics Ph.D. graduates to those in less academically-oriented fields, using both survey and administrative data.
  • Findings:
    • The reform significantly reduced the probability of Economics Ph.D.s entering Italian academia, pushing them toward academic careers abroad or into the public/private sector.
    • This shift disproportionately affected talented graduates, indicating a "negative selection" effect into the Italian academic system.
    • Despite this, tenure-track hires demonstrated higher productivity, publishing more in top-tier journals.
  • Conclusion: The reform's productivity incentives may partially counteract its negative selection effects.
