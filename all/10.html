published: D:20250910211759Z
  • Core Thesis: The paper analyzes the reaction of US bond markets to major AI model releases in 2023-2024 to gauge market belief in "transformative AI."
  • Methodology: It examines changes in long-term US Treasury, TIPS, and corporate bond yields around the dates of significant AI releases. The results are interpreted through a representative agent consumption-based asset pricing model.
  • Key Finding: Major AI releases are associated with statistically significant and economically large decreases in long-term bond yields, with the effect persisting for weeks.
  • Interpretation: According to the asset pricing model, these yield declines suggest that the market, on average, responds to AI advancements by:
    • Revising expected future consumption growth downwards, and/or
    • Reducing the perceived probability of extreme outcomes (such as existential risk or a post-scarcity economy).
  • Ruled-out Alternative: The observed yield changes do not appear to be driven by shifts in uncertainty about consumption growth.
