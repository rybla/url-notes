The Future of Art with AI - Econlib
published: 2025-08-12
  • Law of Demand: As AI lowers the price of creating art, the quantity of art demanded will increase.
  • Diminishing Marginal Utility: The increased demand will primarily serve low-marginal-benefit uses (e.g., personal jokes, blog post illustrations) that were previously not worth the cost of hiring an artist. This creates a new market rather than displacing jobs.
  • Imperfect Substitution: For high-marginal-use art (e.g., commercial products, custom portraits), AI is an imperfect substitute for human artists due to difficulties in communication, interpretation, and achieving specific, nuanced results.
  • Market Bifurcation: The art market is expected to split:
    • A low-marginal-use segment dominated by AI.
    • A high-marginal-use segment where human artists maintain a comparative advantage.
  • Job Impact: Job losses will be concentrated on "intramarginal" artists, whose work is most directly substitutable by AI's capabilities. The overall impact is not predicted to be an existential crisis for the art industry.

28 slightly rude notes on writing
published: 2025-04-29
  • Motive and Cost: Effective writing requires a strong, intrinsic "motive." The writer must answer "Why do you care?" not just "Why should the reader care?" This act of writing is a costly, and therefore meaningful, signal of that care, a quality absent in zero-cost AI-generated text.
  • Thinking vs. Writing: Writing problems are fundamentally thinking problems. Clarity of expression cannot be achieved without clarity of thought. The goal is to operate in the liminal space between the obvious and the obviously wrong.
  • Context over Content: The meaning of text ("the necklace") is derived from its human context ("the neck")‚Äîthe writer's specific circumstances, intent, and sacrifice. AI-generated prose, lacking this "coming-from-ness," is all necklace, no neck.
  • The Nature of Creation: The creative process is described as an unnatural and painful act of forcing precision from the mind. This pain can be addictive, making writing a compulsion for some.
  • Emotion and Sincerity: Bitterness is a sterile emotion for writing because it is self-absorbed. In contrast, writing from despair is an inherent act of hope, a subtextual plea for connection.
  • Legacy and Misinterpretation: A writer's legacy is often outside their control; they are frequently misunderstood or remembered for works they deemed secondary.
  • AI as a Catalyst: Competition from AI presents an opportunity for human writers to focus on their unique advantages: exploring the ineffable, personal experience and "widening the sphere of human sensibility."
